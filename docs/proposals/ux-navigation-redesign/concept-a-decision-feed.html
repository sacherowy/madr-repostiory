Use PostgreSQL for the customer data platform

✅
Decided on 12 May 2026 — decision owned by Marta Kowalska, with input from Platform and Reporting teams.
MKJTAL
In one sentence: we chose PostgreSQL, because our reporting needs relational queries and we already have the skills in-house — accepting that very large event volumes will need an archiving strategy later.

Why we needed to decide MADR: Context and Problem Statement

The customer data platform must store profile, consent and activity data for ~4M customers. Marketing and Support both need to query it, and the current spreadsheet-and-exports approach can’t keep up or pass audit.

What mattered to us MADR: Decision Drivers

· Reporting teams need ad-hoc queries without engineering help  · We must show auditors where every field comes from  · Ops team of two — low operational burden  · Existing team skills

Options we looked at MADR: Considered Options

✓ CHOSENPostgreSQL

Strong reporting, familiar to the team, proven audit tooling.

MongoDB

Flexible schema, but weak fit for cross-entity reporting.

DynamoDB

Lowest ops burden, but locks reporting into export pipelines.

What this means for us MADR: Consequences

Good: reporting can self-serve; audit trail is straightforward. Watch out: activity events may outgrow a single instance — revisit archiving within 12 months.
