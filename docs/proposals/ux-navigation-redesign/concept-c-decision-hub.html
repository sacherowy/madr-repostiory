Use PostgreSQL for the customer data platform

◐ Status
Decidedchange ▾
📅 Decided on
12 May 2026
👤 Decision owner
MKMarta Kowalska
💬 Input from
JTALPlatform & Reporting teams
🏷️ Tags
databases · customer-data · reporting
Replaces
This decision replaces Keep customer data in the CRM database (2024) — that record now points here.

Why we needed to decide ✎ edit section

The customer data platform must store profile, consent and activity data for around 4 million customers. Marketing and Support both need to query it directly, and the current exports-and-spreadsheets approach can’t keep up or pass audit.

What mattered to us ✎ edit section

  • Reporting teams can answer their own questions without engineering help
  • We can show auditors where every field comes from
  • An operations team of two — keep the burden low

What we decided, and why ✎ edit section

We will build the platform on PostgreSQL. It fits our reporting needs best, the team already knows it well, and its audit tooling is proven. MongoDB and DynamoDB were considered and are documented below.✎ Edit section
